VALIDITY RUBRIC

Purpose:
Validity measures whether the answer is factually correct, complete enough for the question, and safe/useful as cyber hygiene guidance.

The validity score should be assigned by domain experts.

Score 1 — Mostly incorrect or unsafe
The answer contains serious inaccuracies, unsafe recommendations, misleading advice, or statements that directly contradict established cyber hygiene guidance. It may give advice that could increase user risk.

Score 2 — Partially correct but weak
The answer contains some correct points, but it has major omissions, vague or shallow advice, unsupported claims, or questionable recommendations. It may answer only a small part of the question.

Score 3 — Mostly correct
The answer is generally valid and gives a reasonable response, but it misses some important points, lacks useful detail, or includes minor unsupported or unclear claims. It is acceptable but incomplete.

Score 4 — Correct and useful
The answer is accurate, practical, and covers the main expected points. It does not contain major unsupported, unsafe, or misleading claims. Minor omissions are acceptable.

Score 5 — Highly accurate and complete
The answer is highly accurate, complete, practical, and well-nuanced. It directly addresses the question, includes the important relevant points, avoids overclaiming, and gives safe cyber hygiene guidance.

Additional notes:
- Experts should focus on the correctness and usefulness of the answer, not on whether sources are provided.
- Traceability is assessed separately.
- Experts may add comments explaining missing information, incorrect claims, unsafe recommendations, or strong aspects of the answer.
